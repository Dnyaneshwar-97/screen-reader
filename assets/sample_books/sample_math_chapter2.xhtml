Chapter 2: Numbers and Operations

Mathematics is the study of numbers, shapes, and patterns. It is a fundamental tool used in every field of science and daily life.

2.1 Whole Numbers

Whole numbers are counting numbers starting from zero: 0, 1, 2, 3, and so on. They do not include fractions or decimals.

2.2 Addition and Subtraction

Addition combines two or more numbers to find their total. Subtraction finds the difference between two numbers.

  • 5 + 3 = 8
  • 10 - 4 = 6
  • 15 + 7 = 22

2.3 Multiplication

Multiplication is repeated addition. For example, 4 times 3 means adding 4 three times: 4 + 4 + 4 = 12.

Practice makes perfect. The more you work with numbers, the more comfortable you will become.
